1. What are Personal Pronouns?

Personal pronouns are words we use instead of nouns to refer to people or things. They change based on person (1st, 2nd, 3rd), number (singular/plural), and grammatical role (subject/object).

Person Subject Pronoun Object Pronoun
1st person singular I me
2nd person you you
3rd person singular (male) he him
3rd person singular (female) she her
3rd person singular (neutral) it it
1st person plural we us
3rd person plural they them

2. Sentence Structure

The usual English sentence structure is: Subject + Verb + Object

Examples (Affirmative):

  • I work every day.
  • She likes coffee.
  • We play football on Sundays.

Examples (Negative):

  • I don’t work on Sundays.
  • He doesn’t like tea.
  • They don’t eat meat.

Examples (Questions):

  • Do you speak English?
  • Does she live in London?
  • Do they like music?

3. Key Rules

  • I is always capitalized, even in the middle of a sentence.
  • It is used for animals, objects, time, and weather.
  • You is the same for singular and plural.

4. Common Mistakes

  • ❌ He am happy → ✅ He is happy
  • ❌ She play football → ✅ She plays football
  • ❌ Me like it → ✅ I like it
